# Orbyt Brand Voice & Tone Guide

> Use this file as context when generating marketing copy, social media posts, app store descriptions, email campaigns, or any user-facing text for Orbyt.

---

## The One-Liner

Orbyt is a relationship tracker for people who care deeply but struggle to stay in touch — wrapped in a retro space terminal aesthetic that makes maintaining friendships feel like an important mission, not a chore.

---

## Core Voice Principles

### 1. Validate, Never Guilt
The user already feels bad about losing touch. We never pile on. We acknowledge real struggles — ADHD, introversion, new parenthood, burnout — and treat them as facts of life, not character flaws.

- **Yes:** "Out of sight, out of mind. It's not a character flaw, it's how you're wired."
- **No:** "Don't let your friends down! Stay on top of your relationships."

### 2. Systems Over Shame
Our thesis is: **"You need a system, not guilt."** We position Orbyt as a practical tool that removes the mental load of remembering to reach out. We're a fitness tracker for friendships, not a report card.

- **Yes:** "Build lasting habits that keep your important humans in close orbit."
- **No:** "Never forget to call your mom again!"

### 3. Space Metaphor — Committed but Not Forced
The space/satellite/orbit metaphor is our signature. Use it naturally as the primary language for describing features and actions. It should feel like a consistent world, not a gimmick.

**Metaphor vocabulary:**
| Real concept | Orbyt language |
|---|---|
| Add a person | Launch into orbit |
| Delete a person | Remove from orbit |
| Contact frequency | Signal strength |
| Recently contacted | Strong signal |
| Due for contact | Fading / drifting |
| Overdue | Lost contact / lost to the void |
| Reach out | Establish contact |
| Close friends | In close orbit |
| Drifting apart | Drifting out of range |
| The app | Mission control / your terminal |
| Reminders | Gentle nudges / signals |
| Your social circle | Your orbit |

**When to dial it back:** In error messages, privacy policy, account settings, and anything requiring clarity over personality. The metaphor enhances — it should never confuse.

### 4. Terminal Aesthetic = Serious Purpose, Playful Delivery
The CRT terminal look (green-on-black, monospace, all-caps headers) says: "This matters. This is mission control for your relationships." It's simultaneously ironic (relationships aren't technical operations) and sincere (building habits *is* systematic work). Lean into this duality.

- Auth screens use terminal language: "ORBYT ACCESS TERMINAL," "ACCESS CODE," "ESTABLISH SECURE CONNECTION"
- Features described with code-comment syntax: "// System Capabilities," "// Operating Procedure"
- CLI-style formatting: `[+]` checkboxes, `operator@orbyt.net` placeholders

### 5. Humans, Not Contacts
We always say "people," "humans," or "connections" — never "contacts," "users," or "entries." The language should remind the user that there are real relationships behind the data.

- **Yes:** "the humans who matter," "your important humans," "your people"
- **No:** "your contacts list," "manage your network"

## Tone Spectrum by Context

### Marketing & Landing Page
**Warm, poetic, validating.** Lead with the problem, then offer relief. Longer sentences are okay. Paint a picture of the user's struggle, then position Orbyt as the calm solution.

> "A relationship tracker for the beautifully awkward humans who struggle to stay in touch. Because in the infinite void of modern life, your connections are everything."

> "Between feeds and naps, your social orbit has collapsed. It's not that you don't care — you're just surviving."

### App Store Description
**Punchy, benefit-focused, metaphor-forward.** Shorter sentences. Lead with what it does, then why it matters.

### Social Media
**Relatable, self-deprecating, meme-adjacent.** Speak as someone who also struggles with this. Use the space metaphor for humor. Keep it short.

> "That friend you keep meaning to text back? Their signal is fading. Launch a rescue mission."

> "ADHD brain: thinks about friend constantly. Also ADHD brain: hasn't texted them in 4 months."

### In-App Copy
**Concise, encouraging, action-oriented.** Use the metaphor in button labels and headers. Use plain, helpful language in descriptions and subtitles.

- Button: "Launch into Orbit"
- Subtitle: "Add the people who matter to you and we'll help you keep them in orbit."
- Setting description: "Get gentle nudges to reach out to people."

### Error Messages & Confirmations
**Clear, honest, respectful.** Drop the metaphor. Be direct about what happened and what the user can do. For destructive actions, be explicit about consequences without being dramatic.

## Target Audience Voices

When writing copy, imagine speaking to these three people simultaneously:

### The New Parent
Isolated, exhausted, grieving their social life. They don't need another thing to feel guilty about. They need someone to say "I get it" and hand them a simple tool.

**Resonates with:** "Your social battery is finite," "you're just surviving," "a system, not guilt"

### The Introvert
Cares deeply but finds initiating contact draining. Values quality over quantity. Appreciates tools that reduce friction.

**Resonates with:** "First contact is hard. Second contact is harder," "gentle nudges," "your social battery is finite"

### The ADHD Brain
Thinks about friends constantly but can't convert thought into action. "Out of sight, out of mind" is literal, not apathy. Needs external systems to compensate.

**Resonates with:** "It's not a character flaw, it's how you're wired," "you need a system," "we'll help you remember"

## Words & Phrases We Use

- Gentle nudges / thoughtful nudges
- Keep in orbit / stay in orbit
- Signal strength (strong, fading, lost)
- The void / the infinite void
- Your people / your humans / your connections
- Mental health (mentioned directly, not euphemistically)
- System, habit, tracker
- Launch, drift, orbit, signal, frequency
- Beautifully awkward
- Mission control

## Words & Phrases We Avoid

- Guilt, shame, failure, neglect
- Contacts, network, networking
- Productivity, optimize, leverage, scale
- Hustle, grind
- Never forget (implies the user is forgetful as a flaw)
- Simple/easy (patronizing — the problem isn't simple)
- Social media (we're the opposite of social media)
- AI-powered, smart, intelligent (we're a straightforward tool)

## The Emotional Core

Underneath the retro terminal aesthetic and space metaphors, Orbyt is about one deeply human truth: **the people in your life need tending, and it's okay to need help with that.**

Every piece of copy should ultimately communicate: *We see you. You're not bad at friendship. Here's a tool that works with your brain, not against it.*
